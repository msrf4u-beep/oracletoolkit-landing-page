Discovery Decision Capture Framework

Every discovery item should produce either a decision, an open question, a dependency, a risk, or a required evidence item.

DecisionWhat was decided, who approved it, and why it matters.
Open QuestionWhat is unresolved and who owns closure before design/build.
DependencyWhich module, configuration, file, integration, or stakeholder is impacted.
RiskWhat can fail later if the discovery item is ignored or delayed.
EvidenceWhat document, test, signoff, or output will prove readiness.
AcceleratorWhich OracleToolkit accelerator or playbook should be used next.
Required Client Inputs

Discovery should not depend on memory.

OracleToolkit should guide teams to request the right inputs early, track missing items, and avoid design decisions based on incomplete client information.

COALegacy COA, segment descriptions, values, hierarchies, reporting examples.
BCEABudget reports, control policies, encumbrance rules, fund hierarchy, grants.
ApprovalsApproval matrix, thresholds, roles, delegation, escalation requirements.
MigrationOpen balances, open transactions, open POs, historical data requirements.
TestingBusiness scenarios, edge cases, signoff owners, expected evidence.
CutoverFreeze windows, fiscal calendar, reconciliation ownership, hypercare process.

Discovery Risk Radar

These are early warning signs that discovery is weak and implementation risk is increasing.

HighCOA decisions keep changingDesign is not stable enough for BCEA, conversion, or reporting build.
HighBCEA ownership is unclearNo one owns budget authority, exception handling, or funds-check decisions.
HighApproval rules are politicalWorkflow may become over-engineered and difficult to maintain.
MediumLegacy data quality is unknownConversion validation and reconciliation effort may be underestimated.
MediumTesting scenarios are genericSIT/UAT may miss public sector, K-12, grant, and encumbrance edge cases.
MediumCutover assumptions are lateFiscal-year close, open POs, budget loads, and reconciliations may collide.
How this becomes operating system behavior

Discovery Command Center connects playbooks, tools, and workspace memory.

The long-term goal is not a static checklist. Discovery decisions should be saved to projects, linked to playbooks, connected to accelerators, and reused during design, testing, migration, and cutover.
